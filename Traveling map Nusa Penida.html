Nusa Penida is a haven for Manta ray enthusiasts, boasting stunning locations and beautiful coral reefs that promise unforgettable experiences. The island is adorned with breathtaking beaches, offering unparalleled beauty that is simply unmatched. Not to be missed is Manta Point, where these majestic creatures reside. Accessing Nusa Penida is convenient with your own transport, departing from the Padang Bay port at 10 am. Alternatively, speed boats from Kusamba or Sanur provide easy access from the south or central areas, respectively.
To begin with, you should know that good places for snorkeling and diving can be found without going tens of kilometers away; they are close to the center, and beautiful beaches are about 30-40 minutes drive from ports and places of main life. Cocopenida opposite this bar there is a coral reef and a very good location for viewing the underwater world, it goes 1 km to the left and 1 km to the right (Video) (Route)
Crystal Bay: A beach known for swimming and snorkeling, offering sun loungers and a cafe. Despite the large crowd and poor toilet facilities, the nearby Secret Beach, just a 150-meter walk away, is stunning and less crowded. You can also explore the nearby Pandan Beach.(Route)
Broken Beach: Not suitable for swimming, but perfect for photography enthusiasts. This spot features a gorge and a huge round platform filled with ocean water, once a favorite spot for Chinese weddings. (Route)
Kelingking Beach: A beautiful beach with a view of the island in the shape of a dinosaur. The descent down to the water is challenging but provides an unforgettable experience. (Route)
Tembeling Beach and Forest: A rocky beach with thermal water in a natural pond, offering views reminiscent of the film "Ten Little Indians." The last kilometer of the road is challenging, so it's recommended for experienced drivers or to walk.(Route)
H&L Sunset Beach: Offers the best view from above on Nusa Penida, but access is difficult. One side features dangerous stairs, while the other has a narrow and broken path.(Route)
Manta Point: Accessible only from the water, but offers a beautiful view of the rocks if you're on land.(Route)
Suwehan Beach: A secluded beach usually with very few people, ideal for a peaceful retreat, especially for couples.(Route)
Diamond Beach and Nusa Penida: Considered the most beautiful beach on Nusa Penida, featuring both a fantastic beach without sunbeds and a cafe, and a calmer side with sunbeds where you can relax and enjoy the beauty of the surroundings.(Route)
